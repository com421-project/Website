Stop unwanted mail

Unwanted mail is a huge waste of natural resources. City of Chicago is joined hands with communities to start a zero waste initiative. Use the free service, designed by Catalog Choice to cancel specific catalogs and other types of paper mail you no longer wish to receive.

Follow the following steps to use this service.
  • Open the following link in any browser on your device: https://chicago.catalogchoice.org/.
  • Click the Sign up button in orange color.
  • Enter your Email ID, name and password and press Sign Up.
  • Check your email account where you will receive an email by Catalog Choice (can be in spam folder)
  • Open the email click on “click here to confirm your account” to activate your account. You will be directed to the login page.
  • To login to your account, you can write your email ID and password to the page you were directed to. Alternatively you can go to the above link and on the top right, you can click on login button (On mobile phones, there will be a bar and options will appear when you click on the bar. Click on login button) After entering your details, click on the login button.
  • Click on “Cancel a Catalog” button and select the paper mail you want to cancel. You can use the alphabets on top row to sort by start letter and the arrow buttons on bottom to go next set of mails. Alternatively, you can use “Search Catalogs” button to search by writing the mail name or a keyword.
  • Click on “Opt out of this catalog” button after selecting the mail.
  • Select profile, name on the label, address on the label, enter catalog details (skip if you don’t have this information) and select the reason to opt out.
  • Click “Confirm” button and you are done.
  • You can check your account info, details, donate by going to specific pages on the website. You can access different pages by clicking on the buttons on top right of the website.

Bike

Bicycling creates no pollution, offers exercise opportunities and is often the quickest way to travel. The Bike 2015 Plan is the City of Chicago’s vision to make bicycling an integral part of daily life in Chicago. The Bike 2015 Plan has two overall goals:
  • to increase bicycle use,
  • to reduce the number of bicycle injuries
Each chapter of the Bike 2015 Plan identifies specific objectives to accomplish the chapter’s overall goal. Chapters include:
  • building a bikeway network
  • Building streets that are bicycle friendly
  • Introduce efficient bike parking
  • connections between bi-cycling and public transport
  • educating about bicycle safety and the benefits of bicycling

Steps to use Divvy for travelling small distances

Using a bicycle for short distances is an efficient way to reduce pollution and it is good for your health. Divvy program offers rent of bikes for a certain duration. Divvy offers D4E (Divvy for Everyone) program that promotes affordable and accessible option for residents of Chicago. Go to the following website: https://www.divvybikes.com/pricing/d4e-chicago Check the features of the program and your eligibility Check the locations below where you can join the program Carry your state ID, $5 cash and visit any office Complete the documentation and you will get a keychain to checkout bicycles. You can also ask for a demo from the representatives in the office. Download the Divvy app to see divvy stations and track distances travelled using divvy.

Before Riding (Safety Checks)

Check the rules on the website to avoid accidents Wear helmets Don’t use the bike if you have an injury Consult a physician/doctor to check if you can ride a bike and you are physically fit. Don’t ride bikes on rainy days.

MeterSave

A program offered by the Chicago Department of Water Management to Chicago where you can save water by participating in MeterSave program. Homeowners without water meters can voluntarily install a meter in their home. Enroll for the program at https://www.metersave.org After you have registered for MeterSave, a representative will call you to schedule your free DWM water meter installation. Sign the consent prior to meter installation You may choose to have your water tested prior to the installation.
